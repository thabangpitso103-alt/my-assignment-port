Basotho Dance Performances

Traditional Dances

Mokhibo

women’s dance performed kneeling with rhythmic clapping.

Mohobelo

men’s group stomping dance, often in unison.

Famo

associated with migrant workers, danced with concertina music.

Setapo

light-hearted youth dance with playful movements.

Ndlamo

a powerful war-inspired dance showcasing energy and bravery.

Phathisi

performed with bells or rattles tied around the ankles.

Ditlhaka

dance involving long sticks, popular during celebrations.

Mokorotlo

a war cry dance symbolizing unity and courage.

Seotho

ceremonial dance for community gatherings.

Kiba

festive dance from migrant workers, later integrated into Basotho styles.

Mokgibo

women’s expressive dance with vocal ululations.

Leeba

solemn dance performed during initiation ceremonies.

Lesiba

a mouth-resonated musical bow producing humming sounds.

Setolo-tolo

a jaw harp played by plucking while held in the mouth.

Moropa

traditional drum used in dances and rituals.

Thomo

another type of stringed musical bow with a resonator.

Sekope

small flute carved from reeds.

Likorotjana

tin can guitars made by young herders.

Konsatīna

adopted into Basotho music, central in Famo style.

Moropa oa letlalo

leather-covered drum for ceremonies.

Dinaka

reed pipes used in group music.

Phalaphala

cow horn trumpet blown at gatherings.

Setsoe

ankle rattles adding rhythm to dances.
